Tourism in Australia

This page demonstrates the number of visitors coming to Australia in 2023 and 2024

Tourism in Australia from 2008 to 2024

The tourism industry in Australia significantly contributes to the economy, generating $36.5 billion and supporting 713,000 jobs in 2021-22. Despite challenges from the COVID-19 pandemic, the sector is recovering and is expected to exceed pre-pandemic levels by 2025. Tourism is vital for regional economies, with a substantial portion of tourism spending occurring in regional areas.

Where did visitors stay in Australia

Visitors to Australia predominantly stay with friends or relatives, accounting for 33% of the total nights spent. Hotels, resorts, motels, and motor inns are the second most popular choice, making up 23% of the total. Rented accommodations follow closely at 21%, while other types of lodging represent 17%. Caravan parks and commercial camping grounds are the least common, with 6% of visitors choosing this option.

The most recent change in number of visitors to each regions

From 2023 to 2024, New South Wales saw a notable increase in visitors from 36,367 to 40,840. Victoria and Queensland also experienced growth, with visitor numbers rising to 31,002 and 27,712, respectively. Other states like South Australia and Tasmania had modest increases, while Western Australia and the Northern Territory remained relatively stable.

Why people travel to different states

Visitors travel to different Australian states primarily for holidays, with New South Wales (NSW) attracting the most holiday visitors. Visiting friends and relatives is another major reason, especially in NSW and Victoria (VIC). Business travel is significant in NSW and Queensland (QLD). Overall, holidays and visiting friends and relatives are the top reasons for travel across all states
